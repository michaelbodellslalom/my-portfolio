Accessibility Strategy Implementation

Implemented new process changes, tools and technology as well as overhauled trainings and documentation in support of new legislation.

CPACC Certification

I hold the Certified Professional in Accessibility Core Competencies (CPACC) credential, issued by the International Association of Accessibility Professionals (IAAP). CPACC is the foundational accessibility certification recognized across industries, validating knowledge of disabilities, assistive technologies, universal design principles, and international accessibility standards including WCAG.

Earning the credential required completing a structured body of study across disability types and their functional impacts, technical and legal accessibility frameworks, and inclusive design strategy — followed by a proctored on-site exam that tested applied understanding across all domains.

Project Overview

Now to the project itself. I applied CPACC knowledge to an organization-wide accessibility strategy, supporting change management, procurement planning, and stakeholder readouts in response to new legislation requirements.

Project Plan

The engagement follows a structured phase-based plan, moving from regulatory discovery and stakeholder alignment through implementation strategy, change management, and content delivery.

  • Rules and Regulations Review: Assess applicable rules and regulations governing state agencies to establish compliance boundaries before evaluation begins.
  • Marketing and Communications Deep Dive: Conduct a thorough review of marketing and communications channels, content types, and ownership to identify accessibility gaps.
  • Change Canvas: Develop and complete a Change Canvas to align stakeholders on the scope, impact, and readiness requirements of the accessibility transition.
  • Stakeholder Deep Dives: Facilitate working sessions across all business teams to understand operational context, surface constraints, and build shared accountability.
  • Application Procurement Implementation Strategy: Document a structured implementation strategy to evaluate third-party applications against accessibility standards as part of the procurement process.
  • Website Implementation Strategy: Scope a website-specific accessibility implementation strategy covering audit methodology, tooling, and remediation prioritization.
  • Change Management Strategy: Complete a comprehensive change management strategy covering communications, training sequencing, and adoption milestones.
  • Initial Process Content: Deliver high-priority process documentation to support immediate operational needs ahead of full rollout.
  • Initial Change and Training Content: Provide high-priority change and training materials to prepare teams for the earliest implementation milestones.

Change Canvas Workshop

The Change Canvas was developed through a facilitated two-part workshop series designed to build shared understanding and alignment across business and program stakeholders before any implementation work began.

Each session was structured to move participants from awareness to active input — surfacing concerns, clarifying roles, and translating accessibility requirements into operational language that teams could act on. Rather than presenting a completed strategy for sign-off, the workshops used collaborative exercises to co-create the canvas, ensuring stakeholder perspectives were embedded in the plan itself.

  • Session 1 focus: Defined the scope of change, mapped impacted teams and roles, and identified the key drivers behind the legislation requirements driving the work.
  • Participants worked through structured prompts to surface what was changing, who would be affected, and what risks or barriers might slow adoption.
  • Outputs informed the initial draft of the Change Canvas and shaped the stakeholder deep dive agenda for subsequent sessions.
  • Session 2 focus: Reviewed and refined the draft canvas with stakeholders, confirmed ownership of key actions, and aligned on the change management and training sequencing.
  • Teams validated the prioritization of process and content changes and confirmed readiness milestones ahead of the Phase 1 readout.
  • The completed canvas became the governing reference for change management activity through the remainder of the engagement.

Deliverables

All deliverables were produced in direct response to new statewide legislation requiring organizations to meet defined accessibility standards across their digital and physical presence. Each output was designed to be actionable — giving teams the guidance, documentation, and governance they needed to execute with confidence.

  • Accessibility Statement: Authored a formal accessibility statement for publication on the client's website and to also be displayed at their physical location. The statement communicates the organization's commitment to accessibility, outlines the standards being met (WCAG AA), and provides contact information for users who encounter barriers.
  • Best Practices Guides: Developed organization-wide best practices guides for updating existing content to meet WCAG AA standards. Guides covered document formatting, image alt text, color contrast, link labeling, video captioning, and form accessibility — written for non-technical staff so teams across the organization could apply standards without relying on specialist support for every update.
  • Implementation Plan and Change Management Guidance: Produced a phased implementation plan mapping compliance milestones, ownership, and sequencing across workstreams. Paired with change management guidance that addressed communications, training rollout, and adoption tracking — ensuring the organization could execute the plan in a structured and sustainable way.

Impact

The phased approach created organizational clarity and readiness ahead of legislation deadlines — aligning stakeholders, establishing procurement guardrails, and delivering the content and strategy teams needed to move forward with confidence. The organization has successfully moved forward into a legislation compliant state.
