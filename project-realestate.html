Project Overview

Partnered with a real estate client to guide the product vision and experience strategy for a new virtual showroom platform — an immersive digital tool designed to help customers design custom homes and evaluate land before purchase.

My Role

I served as the Lead UX Designer, providing human-centered design expertise and collaborating closely with the technical architect to shape the product roadmap and overall experience direction.

Approach

Our approach included the following actions:

  • Stakeholder Interviews and Workshops: Facilitated working sessions to align on business goals, customer needs, and product priorities.
  • Customer Feedback Review: Synthesized existing feedback to deepen understanding of customer profiles and desired experiences.
  • Product Vision and Strategy: Reviewed, facilitated, and guided creation of the product vision to ensure alignment across design and technical workstreams.
  • Roadmap Integration: Collaborated with the Technical Architect to integrate experience trajectory into the broader product roadmap.

Design Style Guide

As part of the engagement, I contributed to a comprehensive design style guide to ensure visual consistency and scalability across the platform. The guide covers:

Typography

Defined type scales and font pairings across display, headline, and body sizes to establish clear visual hierarchy throughout the experience.

Color Guidance

Established a primary palette alongside overlay and zoning-specific colors to support map-based land evaluation and digital wayfinding.

Elevation

Defined shadow and depth standards to create clear layering and spatial relationships between UI components.

Elements

Documented reusable UI components and interactive patterns to support consistent assembly across screens and platforms.

Layout

Specified responsive grid and breakpoint rules across mobile, tablet, and desktop to ensure adaptable, predictable layouts at any screen size.

Figma Component Library

To support both design and development workflows, I built a reusable Figma component library tailored to the platform. Components serve as the single source of truth — enabling designers to assemble screens quickly and giving developers clean, consistent references for implementation.

  • Components are built once and reused across screens, reducing design inconsistency and rework.
  • Each component includes defined states (default, hover, active, disabled) to cover real interaction scenarios.
  • Variants and properties allow a single component to flex across contexts without duplication.
  • Developers reference components during handoff to ensure accurate, spec-aligned builds.

Dev Ready Designs

Before handoff, all screens were prepared as dev-ready designs in Figma — ensuring developers had everything they needed to build accurately without back-and-forth clarification.

  • Screens are fully annotated with spacing, sizing, and layout specifications so developers can build directly from the file.
  • Figma's Dev Mode gives developers inspect access to exact values — colors, typography tokens, and component properties — without needing to ask the designer.
  • Assets and icons are exported and named consistently, reducing ambiguity during implementation.
  • Interactive prototypes linked alongside static specs help developers understand intended behaviors and transitions.

Impact

The engagement established a practical foundation for launching the virtual showroom, improving cross-team alignment and enabling faster, more consistent design-to-development delivery.
