Telecomm

E-Commerce Site Redesign

Led a research-driven modernization of site navigation and core journeys, using prototype validation and prioritized requirements to support successful migration to Adobe Experience Manager.

Project Overview

Partnered with the Telecomm client Digital CoE to shape the redesign strategy and execution for the e-commerce site while advancing toward Adobe Experience Manager (AEM).

Business Need

The initial lift-and-shift to AEM addressed platform migration but left limited redesign in place. At the same time, the e-commerce site grew significantly with additional content and experience complexity. The team needed to optimize navigation, information architecture, and overall usability before broader migration milestones.

Approach

Our approach included the following actions:

  • Define Business Success: Kickoff alignment with stakeholders on CX vision, customer outcomes, and measurable success criteria.
  • Explore Product Experience Opportunity: Mixed-method research including audits, analytics, process mapping, qualitative interviews, and market comparisons.
  • Envision Experience + Solution: Concepting and prototyping with validation, then translation into prioritized requirements, epics, and user stories.

Key OKRs

The strategy prioritized measurable outcomes tied to acquisition, retention, and operational efficiency.

Increase Revenue

Drive growth through stronger acquisition, cross-sell, and upsell performance.

  • Increase conversion rate from baseline to target by an agreed milestone date.
  • Increase cross-brand purchases from web referrals across lines of business.
  • Increase add-on purchases including channel packs, on-demand, and related services.

Increase Retention

Reduce churn risk by improving self-service and reinforcing subscription value.

  • Reduce avoidable contact-center volume through better website self-service.
  • Increase authenticated account adoption to improve first-party engagement.
  • Reduce cancellation-related call behavior after site visits.

Cost Efficiencies

Lower service costs by helping customers resolve support needs online.

  • Reduce support phone calls within days of authenticated web sessions.
  • Simplify navigation to help users find answers without escalating to support.
  • Improve search and support content findability for older demographics.

Prototype

We developed multiple end-to end prototype flows (example picture below) covering homepage and channel shopper, plan and add-ons, equipment selection, and install journey touchpoints.

  • Built a mobile-first fully responsive prototype for walkthroughs with stakeholders.
  • Validated critical journey steps with actual customers to identify friction and comprehension gaps.
  • Iterated on interaction patterns, content clarity, and decision support based on testing insights.
  • Delivered a refined final prototype and design guidance package for implementation handoff.

This concept was tested with real customers and iterated multiple times before final handoff.

Outcomes

The engagement established a clear product north star, improved alignment across teams, and created a repeatable path for experience modernization.

  • Actionable research readouts with personas, journey maps, and service design blueprints.
  • Design concepts and prototypes grounded in validated user needs.
  • Prioritized backlog inputs (epics, features, stories) to accelerate delivery planning.
  • Design system evolution guidance and an ongoing measurement framework.

Deliverables

  • Stakeholder Alignment Workshops
  • Research Plan and Insight Synthesis
  • Personas, Journey Maps, and Service Blueprints
  • Experience Concepts and Prototype Validation
  • Design Metrics and Measurement Plan

Impact

The redesign strategy and validated prototype gave teams a shared direction for modernization, improved confidence in key customer journeys, and accelerated planning for AEM-enabled delivery.
