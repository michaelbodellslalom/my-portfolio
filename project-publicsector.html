Project Overview

Led an end-to-end UX process for a public-facing affordable housing platform on Salesforce Service Cloud and Experience Cloud. The work aligned policy requirements, resident needs, and internal service workflows into one connected experience.

End-to-End UX Process

The project moved through four connected phases: comparative research, user interviews, design testing, and development enablement. Each phase produced decisions and artifacts that informed the next.

Comparative Research

Evaluated housing and civic service portals from peer municipalities and state agencies to identify interaction patterns, accessibility strengths, and service gaps.

  • Benchmarked application flow models, account creation, and status tracking experiences
  • Reviewed WCAG compliance patterns for forms, error handling, and multilingual content
  • Mapped policy and legal requirements to user-facing tasks and backend service operations
  • Identified opportunities to reduce resident drop-off in key completion steps

User Interviews

Conducted resident and staff interviews to ground design decisions in real behavior and operational realities.

  • Residents: gathered insights on digital literacy, trust, document upload challenges, and language barriers
  • Case workers: captured pain points in intake, triage, and communication handoffs
  • Program administrators: validated reporting needs tied to compliance and performance metrics
  • Synthesized findings into personas, journey maps, and prioritized experience principles

Process Flows

Process flows map the step-by-step path a user takes to complete a task — showing screens, decisions, and transitions in sequence. They sit between research and prototyping, turning interview insights into a shared understanding of how the experience should work before any visual design begins. On this project, process flows helped align residents, case workers, and program administrators around a single view of each service journey.

Design Testing

Prototyped and tested end-to-end service journeys before implementation to reduce delivery risk and improve clarity.

  • Built low- and high-fidelity flows for eligibility checks, application submission, and case status updates
  • Ran moderated usability sessions with residents and internal staff
  • Measured task success, completion time, and error frequency across critical steps
  • Iterated information architecture, form structure, and messaging based on evidence

Development and Delivery

Translated validated UX into build-ready specifications and supported implementation from sprint planning through release.

  • Defined component behavior and page templates for Salesforce Experience Cloud
  • Partnered with developers on technical feasibility, QA acceptance criteria, and accessibility validation
  • Aligned Service Cloud workflows to resident-facing states and notifications
  • Supported launch readiness with content governance and post-launch improvement backlog

Solution

A research-led service design approach that connected resident needs to internal service operations, then validated the experience through iterative testing before development.

Deliverables

  • Comparative research summary and opportunity matrix
  • Interview synthesis, personas, and journey maps
  • Validated wireframes and clickable prototypes
  • Design system guidance and accessibility annotations
  • Salesforce Experience Cloud implementation specifications
  • Post-launch optimization roadmap

Impact

The resulting experience created a clearer path from eligibility to submission, reduced service friction for residents, and gave staff a more consistent workflow for intake and case updates.
