Project Overview

Led an end-to-end UX process for a public-facing affordable housing platform on Salesforce Service Cloud and Experience Cloud. The work aligned policy requirements, resident needs, and internal service workflows into one connected experience.

The project moved through four connected phases: comparative research, user interviews, design and testing, finishing with development enablement.

Comparative Research

Comparative research included analysis of platforms like Zillow, Redfin, and state run housing portals.

  • Benchmarked application flow models, account creation, and status tracking experiences
  • Reviewed WCAG compliance patterns for navigation, forms, contact, color and more
  • Identified opportunities to reduce resident drop-off in key completion steps

User Interviews

User interviews saw us meeting with both internal employees and those who have gone through the affordable housing process in the past.

  • Residents: gathered insights on digital literacy, trust, qualification challenges, and technical barriers
  • Program managers: captured pain points in intake, triage, and communication handoffs
  • Project leadership: validated reporting needs tied to compliance and performance metrics
  • Synthesized findings into personas, journey maps, and prioritized design focus areas

Process Flows

Mapped the step-by-step path a Candidate (user) takes to complete a task, whether that be determining their prioritization for affordable housing, viewing potential homes, or submitting an application. We turned interview insights into a shared understanding of how the experience should work before any visual design begins. Process flows helped align candidates, program administrators, and project leadership around a single view of each service journey.

Design and Testing

Design and testing included multiple rounds of Figma design and testing with the user interview groups and project stakeholders.

  • Ran moderated usability sessions with residents and internal staff
  • Measured task success, completion time, and error frequency across critical steps
  • Iterated information architecture, experience patterns, and customer centric language based on learnings

Development Enablement

Development enablement included a design roadmap and dev-ready design series of files. These files included typography and color guidance, reusable components, and more.

  • Defined component behavior and page templates for Salesforce Experience Cloud
  • Partnered with developers on technical feasibility, QA acceptance criteria, and accessibility validation
  • Supported launch readiness with content governance and post-launch improvement backlog

Deliverables

  • Comparative research synthesis and takeaways
  • Interview synthesis
  • Validated wireframes and clickable prototypes
  • Design system guidance and accessibility annotations
  • Salesforce Experience Cloud implementation specifications
  • Post-launch optimization roadmap

Impact

The resulting experience created a clearer path from eligibility for prioritization through submission of a final housing application. We reduced service friction for residents and gave staff a more consistent workflow for prioritization and application intake as well as status tracking and communication.
