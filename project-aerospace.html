Project Overview

Backfilled a key design role on a long-running aerospace product modernization effort, partnering with product management and engineering to re-engineer the front end of a 20+ year-old tool ecosystem. The engagement operated in a traditional sprint model with bi-weekly developer handoffs, active stakeholder design reviews, and downstream implementation by development teams.

Delivery Model

The program followed a structured UX-to-engineering workflow built around predictable sprint ceremonies and frequent alignment points.

  • Traditional sprint structure with joint ceremonies across product, design, and engineering
  • Bi-weekly developer handoffs to keep implementation moving without design ambiguity
  • Ongoing stakeholder design reviews to validate priorities and decisions before build
  • Design work maintained a runway several sprints ahead of development

Context and Team Environment

The product portfolio consisted of more than 170 internal tools with varied maturity levels, significant legacy behavior, and many one-off UI patterns built over years of engineering-led delivery.

  • Mixed team tenure, including contributors who had supported legacy systems for years
  • Historically design-light environment, with engineering carrying most UI decisions
  • Close collaboration with product manager, design leadership, and engineering leads
  • Hybrid operating style: onsite collaboration plus focused heads-down design production

Current-State Assessment

Started with a current-state assessment to evaluate what should be preserved, what should be improved, and what should be redesigned for the future state.

  • Audited existing interfaces and interaction patterns across tools
  • Identified UX and visual design inconsistencies creating user friction
  • Documented immediate improvements versus longer-horizon modernization opportunities
  • Ran weekly design sessions to review findings and align improvement priorities

Information Model and Workflow Clarity

A major workstream focused on improving the information model so users could confidently interpret and act on critical data.

  • Clarified field meaning, intended use, and data relationships across interfaces
  • Made change context explicit, including what changed and who approved updates
  • Reduced ambiguity in high-consequence tasks where precision and traceability matter
  • Aligned updated UI patterns to established operational workflows and governance

Modernization Approach

The engagement prioritized front-end modernization while balancing continuity for users working in established legacy processes.

  • Re-engineered front-end patterns to improve consistency and maintainability
  • Introduced scalable UI decisions to replace one-off components
  • Delivered designs in implementation-ready increments aligned to sprint planning
  • Supported development teams through design QA and iterative review cycles

Timeline and Operating Rhythm

The initial engagement window ran through June 2025, with a multi-year roadmap extending beyond that phase.

  • Kickoff and onboarding initiated immediately to backfill the current design function
  • Knowledge transfer, onboarding coordination, and access readiness prioritized early
  • Planning cadence synchronized with team availability, including alternating Friday schedules
  • Release timing remained variable, requiring strong design runway and discovery discipline

Deliverables

  • Current-state assessment and UX improvement backlog
  • Sprint-ready design packages for bi-weekly developer handoff
  • Information model and field-usage clarity recommendations
  • Modernized front-end patterns and reusable UI guidance
  • Stakeholder review outputs and implementation design QA notes

Impact

The sprint-based model improved alignment between design and engineering, accelerated modernization decisions across legacy tools, and provided clearer, implementation-ready direction for development teams working in a high-precision aerospace environment.
